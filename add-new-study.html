Methods and Procedures

The cattery facility is registered with the USDA No. 23-R-126 under the Animal Welfare Act. The cattery had a 12-hour-light/12-hour-dark cycle. Every attempt was made to keep temperature ranges within targeted conditions (from 50° to 85°F) in accordance with the Animal Welfare Act. Cages, feed bowls, water bowls, and litter boxes were cleaned daily and sanitized in accordance with the Animal Welfare Act.

The purpose of the study was to assess the acceptability of Diet A. The Sponsor owns the study, including raw data, results and final reports. Summit Ridge Farms agrees to keep all aspects of this study and report confidential.

On 02/25/2010, 1 bag of test article Diet A was received.

Twenty male and female Dog identified by ear tattoo and cage number were presented the test diet on an individual basis. Two hundred grams of Diet A were offered in stainless steel bowls once daily for 2 days. Bowls were presented for 4 hours. Total diet consumption was measured and recorded and can be found inTables 1-2.
